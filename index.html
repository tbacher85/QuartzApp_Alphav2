Competitor Feature Gap Analysis

Conduct a competitive analysis of [competitors] in the [product-category] space. Identify 3-5 significant feature gaps or user experience shortcomings that these platforms have based on customer reviews and industry analysis. For each gap, suggest how a new SaaS product could better address these needs.

Pricing Strategy Insight

Analyze the pricing strategies of leading [product-type] SaaS products targeting [target-market]. Identify common pricing model patterns, typical price points for different tiers, and any noticeable gaps in the pricing structures that could be exploited by a new entrant. Suggest an optimal pricing strategy for a new competitor entering this space.

Differentiation Strategy

Include both feature-based differentiators and go-to-market approaches that would help carve out a unique position in this competitive landscape.

Persona Development

Develop 3 detailed buyer personas for [product-type] targeting [business-size]. For each persona, include: demographic information, key responsibilities, pain points, goals, objections to purchasing new software, and preferred communication channels. Also include the specific outcomes they would want from using such a product.

Customer Journey Mapping

Map out the complete customer journey for [user-type] evaluating and purchasing [software-category] solutions. Include: 1) Awareness stage triggers, 2) Consideration phase research behaviors, 3) Decision-making criteria, 4) Onboarding experience expectations, and 5) Retention factors. Highlight key moments where the experience could be improved.

Churn Risk Analysis

For [saas-type] serving [customer-segment], identify the top 5 reasons customers churn based on industry patterns. For each churn reason, suggest: 1) Early warning indicators, 2) Preventive measures the SaaS could implement, and 3) Recovery strategies to win back at-risk customers. Include specific product features or service approaches that could reduce each churn risk.

MVP Feature Prioritization

For a [product-concept] tool targeting [target-user], create a prioritized list of features for an MVP (Minimum Viable Product). Categorize them as: 1) Must-have core features without which the product wouldn't work, 2) Differentiators that set it apart, and 3) Nice-to-have features for future versions. Justify the prioritization based on user needs and technical feasibility.

User Onboarding Flow

Design an optimal user onboarding flow for [software-type] software designed for [technical-level] users. Break it down into stages from initial signup to first meaningful use of the product. Include: 1) Key actions to guide users through, 2) Milestones to celebrate, 3) Potential friction points to avoid, and 4) Metrics to track onboarding success. Suggest specific UI patterns and microcopy that would make this onboarding effective.
